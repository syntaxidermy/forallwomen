We are Sisters Uncut.

We aim to create and protect respectful, understanding and kind spaces where people feel able to express themselves, discuss their experiences, organise, and ask questions without fear of reprisal or humiliation.

Those spaces must be inclusive and supportive spaces, for all women (trans, intersex and cis) and all nonbinary, agender and gender-variant people.

There is a lot of misinformation about trans people being spread by groups like forwomen.scot. Here’s why their scaremongering is misguided.

  • It is often vital to maintain safe spaces to escape gendered oppression, and Sisters Uncut is itself a space where men are not welcome. Trans inclusion has been law in Scotland since the 2010 Equality act, so spaces like crisis centres, women’s shelters, bathrooms, changing rooms etc have been open to all women (cis, trans, intersex and nonbinary) for almost a decade. Including trans women has done nothing to diminish their worth as safe spaces.
  • Confrontation has always been a critical part of the struggle against gender-based oppression. From the suffragettes to today’s trans rights battle, the presence of conflict and anger from those whose rights are limited has always been used to unfairly discredit their struggle.
  • Almost half of all trans people attempt suicide. But studies show that trans kids who are supported by their parents and allowed to transition socially (live as their identified gender) are no more prone to depression than any other children in their peer group. The difference between supporting a child through transition and forcing them back into the closet is literally life and death.
  • Trans people have existed for thousands of years before modern surgical and hormonal treatments. Everyone has a right to bodily autonomy, and nobody’s identity is contingent on whether or not they recieve major medical treatment.

  • Nobody should ever be forced or pressured into sex or relationships with anybody. But people deserve to define their own sexuality - nobody can decide for them. Nobody should have their sexual identity denied just because they or their partner are transgender.
